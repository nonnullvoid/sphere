# Public Identity
*The archetype you're building. Not a persona to perform — a signal to make legible.*

---

## The archetype

*In one sentence: who are you publicly?*

---

## What you want people to feel when they encounter your work

*Not what you want them to think — what you want them to feel. Credibility? Curiosity? Trust? Respect?*

---

## The qualities that define your public presence

*3–5 pairs of qualities that capture the tension in your work — e.g., "expert + experimenter", "intense + unhurried", "technical + philosophical"*

---

## What you are not

*Equally important. What archetypes do you want to avoid? What would feel like a betrayal of your actual identity?*

---

## The arc — where this is going

*Where do you want to be publicly in 1 year? 3 years? What projects, reputation, relationships?*

---

## Projects as signals

*How do your projects collectively build this identity? What does each one add to the picture?*

---

*This document is the north star for every public-facing decision. When in doubt, come back here.*
